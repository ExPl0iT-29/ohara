# Product Vision

## Mission

Build a personal, AI-powered reading companion that becomes the single place where I consume everything I discover on the internet.

This is **not** a bookmark manager.

This is **not** a project management tool.

This is **not** a note-taking application.

It is a **content-first learning system**.

The browser is where I discover things.

This application is where I actually consume, revisit, and understand them.

---

## Philosophy

Every piece of saved content is the primary object.

Projects, tags, AI, search, and organization exist only to enhance the reading experience.

The application should encourage reading rather than collecting.

Every design decision should reduce friction between saving something and actually consuming it.

---

## Product Principles

### Content First

Everything revolves around content.

Articles, videos, PDFs, books, GitHub repositories, documentation, tweets, Reddit posts, research papers, and websites are all treated as first-class content.

Projects never own content.

Content can later be associated with one or many projects, but projects are contextual overlays rather than organizational containers.

---

### Reading First

The application should always optimize for reading.

The home screen should help answer one question:

> "What should I read right now?"

instead of

> "How should I organize my saved links?"

---

### AI Should Work Quietly

AI should reduce manual effort rather than introduce new workflows.

It should automatically:

* identify content type
* extract metadata
* generate summaries
* estimate reading time
* identify topics
* detect related content
* recommend what to read next

The user should rarely have to manually organize anything.

---

### Beautiful by Default

The application should feel closer to Kindle, Spotify, or Netflix than Notion.

Content should be visual.

Cards should contain images, metadata, summaries, and reading progress.

The UI should encourage opening content.

---

### Zero Friction Capture

Saving content should be faster than leaving a browser tab open.

The ideal capture experience is one click.

---

### Library, Not Graveyard

Saved content should never become a forgotten archive.

The application should continuously surface unread content, forgotten content, related content, and contextually relevant material.

---

## Success Metric

The application succeeds if it completely replaces "keeping dozens of Chrome tabs open."

The primary outcome is increased learning, not increased organization.

---

## Guiding Principle

Every feature must answer one question:

"Does this help me learn better?"
